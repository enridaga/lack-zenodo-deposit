# About LACK

**LACK (Lobbying Against Climate Knowledge)** is a research project developing a knowledge graph of the individuals and organisations involved in lobbying against climate science awareness and climate policy. It is part of the [ClimateSense](https://github.com/climatesense-project) project at the Knowledge Media Institute (KMi).

The knowledge graph is built from a large corpus of real-world relation expressions extracted from investigative journalism sources, and is described using a purpose-built lightweight ontology.

---

## Research Questions

The knowledge graph is designed to support investigation into three interconnected themes.

### Transparency and Disclosure

- **Who is lobbying?** Which specific individuals, companies, trade associations, or coalitions are involved in lobbying efforts?
- **How are they funded?** What are the funding sources for lobbying groups, and are those sources transparently disclosed?
- **Who are they meeting with?** Which policymakers, government officials, or other key actors are they engaging with?
- **How transparent are their activities?** Is there a publicly accessible record of lobbying activities, meeting minutes, and the specific policies discussed?

### Alignment with Science and Policy

- **Are their positions consistent with climate goals?** Do lobbying positions align with the scientific consensus and the goals of international agreements like the Paris Agreement?
- **What specific policies are they advocating for or against?** Renewable energy targets, fossil fuel subsidies, carbon pricing?
- **Do they acknowledge the science?** Do lobbyists acknowledge that climate change is real, human-caused, and poses significant risks, or do they spread misinformation?

### Influence and Impact

- **What is their strategy?** Direct lobbying (meetings with officials) or indirect lobbying (PR campaigns, social media, funding think tanks)?
- **Who are the "trusted messengers" they use?** Are they using scientists, weather presenters, or other seemingly authoritative voices?
- **How is their influence measured?** What evidence is there of lobbying impacting policy decisions or the implementation of climate action plans?

## Sources

LACK is built primarily from two investigative sources:

| Source | Coverage |
|---|---|
| [Desmog](https://www.desmog.com/) | 943 person and organisation profile pages |
| [LobbyMap / InfluenceMap](https://lobbymap.org/) | 964 entries (league table) + 338 linked by |

Additional candidate sources are under consideration. 

---

## Status

LACK is an active research project. Version 1.0 of the knowledge graph was released on 9 April 2026, comprising. The project is currently a resource paper submission to [ISWC 2026](https://iswc2026.semanticweb.org/).
